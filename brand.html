01

The brand

littleL&D is a bedside companion for labor & delivery nurses. The brand reflects what good L&D care feels like: warm, attentive, calm, prepared.

What we are

A quiet, well-organized digital checklist that lives on a phone or tablet at the bedside. The brand should feel like a trusted coworker — competent, kind, slightly playful, never alarming. It's clinical enough to belong on the unit, soft enough to ease a 12-hour shift.

Personality traits

Warm

Soft pastels, rounded shapes, italic flourishes. Nothing sharp or sterile.

Calm

Generous whitespace, restrained typography. Never noisy or flashy.

Capable

Editorial serif + clean sans. Precise without being cold.

Quietly playful

Flower markers, subtle smile of an italic word. Joy without cute overload.

What we're not

  • Not corporate. No grey gradients, no stock-photo professionalism, no acronyms in caps.
  • Not infantilizing. No bottles, pacifiers, baby blocks. The audience is highly skilled professionals.
  • Not alarming. No red urgency, no flashing indicators, no clinical iconography.
  • Not generic. No system fonts, no "wellness app" purple gradients, no rounded blue checkboxes.
02

Logo system

The mark is a fetal heart-rate trace (FHR) that ends in a small bloom — a visual any L&D nurse recognizes instantly. The wordmark pairs an italic Fraunces "little" with a bold roman "L&D" for tension between flow and precision.

Primary lockup

Use on light backgrounds, marketing materials, the homepage header.

On dark backgrounds

App icon

Light variant — 120px
Standard — 80px
Dark variant — 80px

Compact mark

Use in app headers, narrow spaces, browser tab favicons.

Clear space & minimum size

Clear space: Maintain padding equal to the height of the bloom around all sides of the lockup. Minimum size: 120px wide on screen, 0.75" wide in print. Below that, use the compact mark or the icon alone.

04

Typography system

Two typefaces — both free on Google Fonts. Fraunces brings warmth and personality through its italic curves; Nunito brings clarity and reliability through its rounded sans-serif geometry.

Display · Headlines · Section titles
Fraunces italic
Family: Fraunces · Weights used: 400 (italic), 500, 600, 700
Tracking: -0.02em on display, -0.01em on subheadings
Italics: always paired with romans for tonal contrast
Google Fonts: fonts.google.com/specimen/Fraunces
Body · UI · Buttons · Captions
Nunito carries the everyday — labels, buttons, body copy. Soft, rounded, kind.
Family: Nunito · Weights used: 400, 500, 600, 700
Body size: 16px / 1rem · Line height: 1.6
UI elements: 0.85–0.92rem
Uppercase labels: tracking 0.08–0.12em
Google Fonts: fonts.google.com/specimen/Nunito

Hierarchy

Section title — Fraunces 36px
Sub title — Fraunces 21px
Card title 🌸 Fraunces 18px
Body text — Nunito 16px regular, line-height 1.6, for paragraphs and checklist items.
UPPERCASE LABEL — NUNITO 12PX, TRACKING 0.12EM
The italic flourish: The signature move is pairing italic Fraunces with roman Fraunces in the same heading — "Brand guidelines". The italic word is the warm/personal one; the roman is the firm one. Use sparingly — once per major heading is enough.

06

Voice & tone

How we talk to nurses across the app, marketing, documentation, and any future communications.

Principles

Clear, not curt

A checklist item should land in a single glance. Cut filler words but keep human warmth.

Trusting, not handholding

Nurses are experts. Never explain clinical concepts. Reference workflows by their working names.

Calm, not chirpy

No exclamation marks in UI. Save the warmth for tiny moments — a "✿" in the header, an italic flourish in a title.

Imperative, not pleading

"Check prenatal labs" not "Don't forget to check prenatal labs." Direct verbs, no "please."

Examples

In our voice

"Each shift, a fresh start."

"Mark off the times you completed checks."

"A bedside companion for every shift."

Off-brand

"Get ready to crush your shift! 💪"

"Please remember to mark all the times!!"

"Your ultimate L&D productivity solution."

07

Do & don't

Quick rules to keep everything looking like littleL&D — across screenshots, marketing pages, social posts, and documentation.

Do

Use the italic-roman pairing for headers ("Brand guidelines").

Don't

Italicize everything or make every heading the same.

Do

Keep backgrounds soft pastel or warm paper.

Don't

Use pure white, pure black, or saturated brand colors as full backgrounds.

Do

Use room markers as pictograms — small, contained, decorative.

Don't

Blow markers up to hero size or use them as primary brand symbols.

Do

Use the FHR-bloom mark as the consistent brand symbol everywhere.

Don't

Recolor, redraw, or substitute the FHR-bloom mark with another flower.

Do

Use generous padding inside cards — 1.5rem minimum.

Don't

Cram content edge-to-edge. Whitespace is part of the brand.

Do

Use emoji "✿" or "＋" as tiny accents in titles/labels.

Don't

Use generic emoji (🎉 🚀 💯 ⭐) in product or marketing copy.
